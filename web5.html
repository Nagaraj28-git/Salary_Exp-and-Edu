Carrer growth and Advancement

1.Entry-Level Developer Initial Role:As an entry-level developer, you’ll start by gaining hands-on experience with coding, debugging, and understanding the software development lifecycle.

Typical Responsibilities: Writing and testing code Debugging and troubleshooting

2. Junior Developer With a couple of years of experience, you’ll progress to a Junior Developer role, where you’ll take on more responsibility and start contributing to larger projects.

Typical Responsibilities: Working on more complex features and modules Collaborating with cross-functional teams

3. Mid-Level Developer As a Mid-Level Developer, you’ll become more proficient and begin to take ownership of significant projects. This role involves more decision-making and leadership within your development team

Typical Responsibilities: Leading smaller projects or teams Designing and implementing new features

4. Senior Developer Leadership Role:Senior Developers have extensive experience and a deep understanding of software design and architecture

Typical Responsibilities: Leading large-scale projects and initiatives Designing complex systems and architecture

Education Qualification

A Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science , Software Engineering, or a related field is typically required.
Advanced degrees or specialized certifications can be beneficial
